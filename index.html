Good Questions

FAQ

What is Top of the Ops?
Top of the Ops is a podcast hosted by Cecilia Manduca and Beatrice Aliprandi, two former VCs turned operators. Each episode explores the unglamorous, fascinating reality of running the show from behind the scenes: ops, strategy, Chief of Staff life, and everything in between.
Who hosts Top of the Ops?
It's co-hosted by Cecilia Manduca (former VC at Talis Capital, Chief of Staff at Seqera, and FD at PortalOne) and Beatrice Aliprandi (former VC at Lakestar, now Chief of Staff at Praktika).
Where can I listen to Top of the Ops?
Every episode is on YouTube and Spotify. Full show notes and transcripts live right here on this site.
What topics does Top of the Ops cover?
Operations, Chief of Staff roles, startup strategy, managing up, cross-functional leadership, AI and automation in ops, and the behind-the-scenes reality of scaling organisations. It's made for ops professionals, Chiefs of Staff, and anyone in an invisible infrastructure role.
What does a Chief of Staff actually do?
A Chief of Staff is a senior operator who works directly with a CEO or founder, switching between strategy and execution: running planning cycles, fixing broken processes, preparing board materials, and acting as a trusted challenger. We dedicate whole episodes to this, including episode 5, where we walk through our real week-by-week schedules, and episode 7, on the eight ways the role fails.
When should a startup hire a Chief of Staff?
Our view, discussed in episode 4 and episode 7: the sweet spot is usually Series A, when there's early revenue, the company is starting to scale, and the systems for scaling are still being shaped. Pre-seed and seed are usually too early, and by Series B the scope often narrows because functional hires already exist.
